About Oxygen Font

The Oxygen font is a new typeface family being developed for use with the KDE desktop and other GNU/Linux user interfaces.

The aim designing the Oxygen Fonts is to create a new high quality font family for the GNU/Linux communities expand the choice of fonts suitable for use with Free Software desktops and devices.

A new font for Free Desktops and GUIs

The Oxygen fonts are being developed in wide range of weights including the development of 'Oxygen Mono', a monospaced font for use in terminals and for coding.

Free Software

The Oxygen Fonts are published under the Open Font License and the Gnu Public License. These licenses ensure that the fonts can be shared and distributed freely by everyone. The fonts can be downloaded, printed in commercial and non-commercial works, served as webfonts, embedded in documents, software and hardware.

Oxygen Fonts are designed to be as free as possible.

Funding & Roadmap

The Oxygen Fonts have been in progress for over 12 months.

It is now planned to raise development funding via the crowd funding site indiegogo, so that the fonts can be completed with concentrated quality effort.

Six weights of Oxygen Sans are planned; Thin, Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, Heavy, plus italics, and two weights of Oxygen Mono.

Another aim of the next phase of the development of the Oxygen Fonts is to provide unicode support for full Latin, Cyrillic and Greek scripts.
